UNIT - I: Thermodynamics Fundamentals

1. Basic Concepts of Thermodynamics

Thermodynamics is the branch of physics that deals with the relationships between heat and other forms of energy.

Key Point: A thermodynamic system is any collection of matter within prescribed and identifiable boundaries.

Types of Systems:

  • Open System: Mass and energy can cross the boundary
  • Closed System: Only energy can cross the boundary
  • Isolated System: Neither mass nor energy can cross the boundary

2. Zeroth Law of Thermodynamics

If two systems are in thermal equilibrium with a third system, they are in thermal equilibrium with each other.

Importance: This law provides the basis for temperature measurement.

3. First Law of Thermodynamics

Energy cannot be created or destroyed, only transformed from one form to another.

ΔU = Q - W

Where:
ΔU = Change in internal energy
Q = Heat added to system
W = Work done by system

4. Second Law of Thermodynamics

The entropy of an isolated system never decreases; it either increases or remains constant.

dS ≥ dQ/T

For reversible processes: dS = dQ/T
For irreversible processes: dS > dQ/T
Applications: Heat engines, refrigerators, and heat pumps all operate based on the second law.

5. Entropy

Entropy is a measure of the randomness or disorder of a system.

Properties of Entropy:

  • It is a state function
  • It increases for irreversible processes
  • It remains constant for reversible adiabatic processes
  • It can be used to determine the direction of spontaneous processes

6. Work and Heat

Work and heat are both forms of energy transfer, but they differ in their nature:

Work (W):

  • Organized energy transfer
  • Can be completely converted to other forms
  • Boundary work: W = ∫ P dV

Heat (Q):

  • Disorganized energy transfer
  • Occurs due to temperature difference
  • Cannot be completely converted to work (Kelvin-Planck statement)
Study Tips:
  • Practice solving problems involving PV diagrams
  • Understand the difference between state and path functions
  • Memorize important formulas and their applications
  • Focus on conceptual understanding of entropy
