Theory of Relativity & Laser Physics

Special Theory of Relativity

Postulates of Special Relativity

  1. The laws of physics are the same in all inertial reference frames
  2. The speed of light in vacuum is constant for all observers
Lorentz Transformation:
x' = γ(x - vt)
t' = γ(t - vx/c²)
Where: γ = 1/√(1 - v²/c²)

Relativistic Effects

Time Dilation: Δt' = γΔt
Length Contraction: L' = L/γ
Mass-Energy: E = mc²

Velocity Addition

In relativistic mechanics, velocities don't add linearly.

v = (v₁ + v₂)/(1 + v₁v₂/c²)

Laser Physics

LASER

Light Amplification by Stimulated Emission of Radiation

Einstein Coefficients

  • A₂₁: Spontaneous emission coefficient
  • B₂₁: Stimulated emission coefficient
  • B₁₂: Absorption coefficient
Einstein Relations:
B₁₂ = B₂₁
A₂₁ = (8πhν³/c³)B₂₁

Population Inversion

Essential condition for laser action where more atoms are in excited state than ground state.

N₂ > N₁ (Population inversion condition)

Laser Characteristics

  • Coherence: Temporal and spatial
  • Monochromaticity: Single wavelength
  • Directionality: Highly collimated beam
  • High Intensity: Concentrated energy

Laser Examples

He-Ne Laser

Gas laser using helium-neon mixture, produces red light at 632.8 nm.

Ruby Laser

Solid-state laser using chromium-doped aluminum oxide, produces red light at 694.3 nm.

Applications

  • Medical: Surgery, therapy, diagnostics
  • Industrial: Cutting, welding, engraving
  • Communications: Fiber optic systems
  • Research: Spectroscopy, interferometry
  • Defense: Rangefinding, guidance systems
