Applied Physics 1 - Practice Problems

UNIT - I: Heat and Work Problems

Problem 1: First Law Application

Question: A gas undergoes a process in which 500 J of heat is added to the system and the gas does 200 J of work. Calculate the change in internal energy.

Solution:

Given: Q = 500 J, W = 200 J
Using First Law: ΔU = Q - W
ΔU = 500 - 200 = 300 J

Answer: The change in internal energy is 300 J.

Problem 2: Entropy Calculation

Question: Calculate the entropy change when 1 kg of water at 100°C is converted to steam at 100°C. (Latent heat of vaporization = 2.26 × 10⁶ J/kg)

Solution:

Given: m = 1 kg, L = 2.26 × 10⁶ J/kg, T = 373 K
ΔS = Q/T = mL/T
ΔS = (1 × 2.26 × 10⁶)/373 = 6061 J/K

Answer: The entropy change is 6061 J/K.
