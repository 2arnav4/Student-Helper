Introduction to Thermodynamics

Fundamental Concepts

System

A thermodynamic system is a specific portion of matter with a definite boundary on which our attention is focused. Everything outside the system is called surroundings.

State

The condition of a system at any instant of time as described by its properties is called the state of the system.

Process

When a system changes from one state to another, it is said to have undergone a process.

Equilibrium

A system is said to be in thermodynamic equilibrium when no change in any macroscopic property is observed.

Laws of Thermodynamics

Zeroth Law of Thermodynamics

If two systems are in thermal equilibrium with a third system, then they are in thermal equilibrium with each other.

First Law of Thermodynamics:
ΔU = Q - W
Where: ΔU = Change in internal energy, Q = Heat added, W = Work done by system
Second Law of Thermodynamics:
dS ≥ 0 (for isolated system)
Where: S = Entropy
