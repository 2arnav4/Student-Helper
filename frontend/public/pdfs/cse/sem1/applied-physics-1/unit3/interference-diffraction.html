Interference, Diffraction & Polarization

Interference

Interference

The phenomenon of superposition of two or more coherent light waves resulting in regions of enhanced and diminished illumination.

Young's Double Slit Experiment

Demonstrates wave nature of light through interference pattern formation.

Fringe Width:
β = λD/d
Where: λ = wavelength, D = distance to screen, d = slit separation

Newton's Rings

Circular interference fringes formed between a curved surface and a flat surface.

Ring Radius:
r_n = √(nλR)
Where: n = ring number, R = radius of curvature

Diffraction

Fraunhofer Diffraction

Diffraction pattern observed at large distances from the diffracting aperture.

Single Slit Diffraction:
a sin θ = nλ (for minima)
Where: a = slit width, θ = diffraction angle

Diffraction Grating

Multiple parallel slits that produce sharp diffraction maxima.

Grating Equation:
d sin θ = nλ (for maxima)
Resolving Power: R = nN
Where: d = grating spacing, N = total number of slits

Polarization

Polarization

Phenomenon in which vibrations of light waves are restricted to specific directions.

Malus' Law:
I = I₀ cos²θ
Where: I₀ = incident intensity, θ = angle between transmission axes
Brewster's Law:
tan θₚ = n₂/n₁
Where: θₚ = polarizing angle, n = refractive indices

Key Applications

  • Optical Instruments: Resolving power calculations
  • Interference Filters: Wavelength selection
  • Holography: 3D image recording
  • Polarizing Filters: Glare reduction
  • LCD Displays: Liquid crystal technology
