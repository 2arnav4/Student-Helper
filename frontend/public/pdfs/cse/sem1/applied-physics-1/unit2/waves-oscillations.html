Waves and Oscillations

Simple Harmonic Motion (SHM)

Definition

A motion that repeats itself in equal intervals of time and follows Hooke's law.

SHM Equation:
x = A sin(ωt + φ)
Where: A = Amplitude, ω = Angular frequency, φ = Phase constant
Angular Frequency:
ω = 2πf = 2π/T
Where: f = Frequency, T = Time period

Wave Motion

Wave Equation

The general wave equation describes the propagation of waves through a medium.

Wave Equation:
∂²y/∂t² = v² ∂²y/∂x²
Where: y = Displacement, v = Wave velocity
Wave Velocity:
v = fλ = ω/k
Where: λ = Wavelength, k = Wave number

Electromagnetic Theory

Maxwell's Equations

Four fundamental equations that describe electromagnetic phenomena.

Gauss's Law: ∇·E = ρ/ε₀
Gauss's Law for Magnetism: ∇·B = 0
Faraday's Law: ∇×E = -∂B/∂t
Ampère's Law: ∇×B = μ₀J + μ₀ε₀∂E/∂t

Key Topics

  • Superposition Principle: Linear combination of waves
  • Standing Waves: Interference of waves traveling in opposite directions
  • Electromagnetic Waves: Self-propagating waves of E and B fields
  • Poynting Vector: Energy flow in electromagnetic fields
  • Wave Propagation: How waves travel through different media
